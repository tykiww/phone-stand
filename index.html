The Paper Phone Stand

Cheap Thrills for Real Deals

Created by the Dunder Mifflin Paper Co.

Quality cheap phone stands are a dime a dozen. However, with a sheet of origami paper, impossible is nothing.

Get ready to enjoy our 'must-have' innovation of the century!

Materials

1. Square/Origami paper (~8in by 8in)

2. Your very own smartphone

3. Mad folding skills!

Click > below to begin!

Step 1

Make a crease, splitting the paper vertically (hot-dog style), and open the paper back up.

Step 2

Fold the outside edges in until they meet together at the previously created crease. Make sure to fold down the edges.

Step 3

Flip the paper over and bring the bottom edge up to match the top edge, folding and creating a crease in the middle.

Step 4

Open the last fold, then bring the top and bottom edges in until they meet together at the crease (as in step 2). Crease and then unfold.

Step 5

Flip the paper over and make two diagonal folds in the middle square section. When done and opened up, the creases should make an X pattern in the middle square.

Step 6

Take one end of the paper and fold under the corners equally to make a pointed top with the tip directly on the crease. Unfold these to create two small triangles at the top.

Step 7

Place thumb and index finger one of the long edges in the middle box and bring them together, puckering the paper into a triangle in the middle. Fold this down to one side.

Step 8

Following the arrow, tuck the edge into the inside most point. Press downward on this fold, matching the pre-existing creases until flat This should create a pyramidal cavity with a puckered diamond at one corner.

Step 9

Press the protruding diamond on the crease, collapsing it into the inner crease of the cavity. Notice, you are almost done!

Step 10

Now take the protruding rectangular flap and fold it under, in half.

Step 11

Lastly, flip the pyramid right-side up and fold the rectangular flap in half again. Place phone on stand and watch a video!

Hooray! You've finished
